Key insights & strategic recommendations

Revenue growing but AOV declining — volume masking a pricing problem

Revenue grew 54% from 2015 to 2018, but AOV fell from $521 to $458 (−12%). The business scales by order volume, not ticket size. Recommendation: product bundles, upsell flows at checkout, and minimum-order incentives targeting the Corporate segment.

Champions (197 customers) drive 41% of total revenue

The top RFM tier generates $1M from just 25% of the base. A structured loyalty program for this cohort protects a disproportionate share of revenue. Meanwhile, the 186 "Potential" customers ($407K) are primed for conversion — highest-leverage re-engagement target.

Strong Q3/Q4 seasonality — Q1/Q2 demand consistently under-served

Revenue spikes in September and November across all four years while January–February underperform by 60–70%. Recommendation: fiscal year-start Corporate campaigns and loyalty rewards redeemable in Q1 to smooth the revenue curve and reduce cash-flow risk.

South region generates only 17% of revenue — opportunity or structural gap?

West ($732K) and East ($670K) dominate while South lags at $415K. Before investing in expansion, a region-specific cohort analysis and competitive audit are needed to determine whether the gap is driven by distribution coverage, product mix, pricing, or demand.
